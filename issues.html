Issues that Impact Women

Below are three main issues that have been identified as important for discussion amongst our politicians. This is by no means an exhaustive list but serves as a framework to find our collective voice. 

Healthcare

Access to quality affordable health care opportunities for all

Access to affordable and comprehensive healthcare for women is a major political issue. Despite significant progress, the Affordable Healthcare Act (ACA) reforms remain unavailable to women for whom health insurance itself is out of reach. Additionally women are at higher risk of being uninsured, making access to healthcare an urgent issue. And even women who do have insurance higher prices and uncertainty of coverage. What are the issues women have to consider when it comes to comprehensive, affordable healthcare for themselves and their family? How have the 2016 presidential candidates addressed this crucial issue?

Economic Opportunities

Reduction of barriers that inhibit equitable participation in the economy

Growing economic inequality and income gaps is a major concern for all Americans. Women have suffered steep declines in take-home pay and employment, and a faster rise in poverty than men since the Great Recession, especially among single mothers of children under age 18 (39.8%). Solutions that lift women out of poverty and ensuring they are fully able to contribute to the economy, may include access to quality affordable childcare, paid family leave, affordable education, and fairer taxes and financial policies.

Personal Safety

Safe and just recourse for women and girls when it comes to violence in the home, on campus, in the military and in prisons.

Violence against women in the United States occurs in many forms, including domestic violence, human trafficking, sexual assault on campus and in the military. More can be done to provide support for survivors, hold perpetrators accountable, challenge sexism that perpetuates violence, and show leadership on the international stage to end violence against women in all forms. How should politicians address the violence that impacts women, children and families in the United States?
