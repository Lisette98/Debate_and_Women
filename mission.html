Mission

The Women’s Debate serves as a platform to convene a collective voice for women’s rights leading up to the 2016 federal election. We call upon all political candidates to commit to a presidential debate focused exclusively on issues affecting American women, namely:

  • Safe and just recourse for women when it comes to violence in the home, on campus, in the military, and in prisons;

  • Reduction of barriers that inhibit equitable participation in the economy; and

  • Access to quality affordable health care opportunities for all.

It is our vision that gender safety and equality becomes a reality in all spheres of public and private life.

By elevating these issues and introducing them into the national presidential debate, it can help to raise awareness and begin to reduce gender inequality.

Who We Are

The Women’s Debate (“TWD”) was created in the fall of 2015 by a team of leaders in the fields of traditional and social media, Corporate America, entrepreneurial organizations, and non-profit associations. We educators, experts, and concerned citizens in the fields of healthcare, violence, workplace environments and families. We are all working towards a common goal – an equitable and safe environment for all.

Understand the Issues

Women represent more than half the population and turn out to vote more than men yet women’s issues, outside of Planned Parenthood funding, have received little attention during the presidential debates.

From domestic and institutional violence, access to healthcare, economic participation, the pay gap or sexual harassment—these are issues that affect women and girls and should matter to all Americans.

What You Can Do

Please answer our survey to tell us which issues matter to you most and submit the questions you want answered by the candidates.

Whether for yourself, your daughter, your spouse/significant other, or because it is the right thing to do, we urge you to raise questions related to gender equality and women’s rights to be addressed during this important election year.
