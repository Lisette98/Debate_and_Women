About

The Women's Debate ("TWD") was created in the fall of 2015 by a team of leaders in the fields of traditional and social media, Corporate America, entrepreneurial organizations, and non-profit associations. TWD aims to reduce gender inequality by deploying a presidential debate to discuss issues specifically affecting women in America. TWD hopes that by elevating these issues and introducing them into the national presidential debate, it can help to raise awareness and begin to reduce gender inequality.

TWD is seeking input to enable it to convene questions for our political candidates. This initiative will generate solutions proposed by these candidates to advance women in three areas: Healthcare, Economic Opportunities and, Personal Safety.

Rather than duplicate the efforts of leaders and organizations that serve women today, TWD will facilitate a common platform among existing organizations, whether for-profits, non-profits, NGOs and academic institutions, working together to encourage the government to join them in effecting change. No organization can do this by itself.
