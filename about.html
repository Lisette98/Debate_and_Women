About

The Women's Debate ("TWD") was created in the fall of 2015 by a team of leaders in the fields of traditional and social media, Corporate America, entrepreneurial organizations, and non-profit associations. TWD calls for the need for a presidential debate focused exclusively on issues affecting American women. TWD hopes that by elevating these issues and introducing them into the national presidential debate, it can help to raise awareness and begin to reduce gender inequality. 

This initiative will generate solutions proposed by presidential candidates to advance women in three areas: safe and just recourse for women when it comes to violence in the home, on campus, in the military and in prisons; reduction of barriers that inhibit equitable participation in the economy, including accommodation for pregnant and working mothers, and access to health care opportunities that ensure marginalized female populations are taken care of. 

Rather than duplicate the efforts of leaders and organizations that serve women today, TWD will facilitate a common platform among existing organizations, whether for-profits, nonprofits, NGOs, and academic institutions working to encourage the government to effect change.  No one organization can do this by itself.

Understand the Issues

Women represent more than half the population and turn out to vote more than men yet women’s issues, outside of Planned Parenthood funding, have received little attention during the presidential debates.

From domestic and institutional violence, access to healthcare, economic participation, the pay gap or sexual harassment—these are issues that affect women and girls and should matter to all Americans.
