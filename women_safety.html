Women and Issues of Safety

The United Nations defines violence against women as "any act of gender-based violence that results in, or is likely to result in, physical, sexual or mental harm or suffering to women, including threats of such acts, coercion or arbitrary deprivation of liberty, whether occurring in public or in private life."

Violence against women continues to be an epidemic, even today. 85% of all domestic violence victims are women, and 1 in 3 have been the victim of some physical violence by an intimate partner.
